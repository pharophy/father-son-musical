# Before Your Name - Lyrics

## Verse 1

I tightened every screw on the secondhand bed  
Counted every cloud on the wall overhead  
Folded little shirts that could fit in my hand  
Like folding up a map to an unknown land  

I tuned the old guitar till the room rang clear  
Played a line and left a little space right here  
Not a sound came back, but I played it again  
Like maybe you were listening then  

## Verse 2

Your books are on the shelf with the spines still new  
There is a night-light moon and a painted blue  
There is a drawer of socks, there is a rocking chair  
There is everything I know how to put there  

But I cannot build the laugh you will have someday  
Cannot mark the path you will take away  
Cannot make a rule that will keep you safe  
From every wind that comes through this place  

## Chorus

Before your name, before your cry  
Before I learn the color of your eyes  
I am playing to the quiet  
I am waiting for the flame  
I am loving someone missing  
I am calling through the frame  
Before your hands can hold me  
Before I know what I became  
I am yours before your name  

## Bridge

What if I am only strings and wood  
What if I do not know the words I should  
What if all my careful plans fall through  
The first time I have to answer you  

So I will leave a little room inside the song  
For the note I cannot teach, but you bring along  
If I start too sure, if I play too loud  
May I learn to lay the music down  

## Final Chorus

Before your name, before your cry  
Before the world can tell me who arrives  
I am playing to the quiet  
I am waiting just the same  
I am loving someone missing  
I am calling through the frame  
There is one line I can give you  
There is one I cannot claim  
I am yours before your name

## Dramatic Turn

The father begins by trying to prepare perfectly. By the end, he accepts that the song must contain space for a child he cannot control or fully imagine.
