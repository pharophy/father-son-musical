# Before Your Name - Vocal Melody

## Scope

This is a guide melody for the father vocal over the current chord map. It is written to be singable, restrained, and slightly searching. The melody rises when the father imagines the child and falls when he admits what he cannot control.

## Tonal Center

- Key center: C major
- Meter: 4/4
- Tempo: 100 BPM
- Range target: C4 to D5
- Delivery: intimate baritone/tenor, conversational but pitched

## Verse Motif

Use mostly stepwise motion. Leave space after each two-line thought.

```text
I tightened every screw on the secondhand bed
C4 D4 E4 E4 | G4 G4 E4 -

Counted every cloud on the wall overhead
D4 E4 F4 F4 | G4 A4 G4 -

Folded little shirts that could fit in my hand
E4 F4 G4 G4 | A4 B4 C5 -

Like folding up a map to an unknown land
C5 B4 A4 G4 | F4 E4 D4 -
```

## Chorus Motif

The hook should open upward, then settle back down on "before your name."

```text
Before your name, before your cry
F4 G4 A4 A4 | C5 C5 A4 -

Before I learn the color of your eyes
G4 A4 B4 C5 | D5 C5 B4 -

I am playing to the quiet
A4 G4 F4 E4 | G4 A4 G4 -

I am yours before your name
E4 G4 A4 G4 | E4 D4 C4 -
```

## Bridge Motif

Use smaller intervals and lower register. The father is no longer performing readiness; he is admitting fear.

```text
What if I am only strings and wood
A3 C4 D4 E4 | F4 E4 D4 -

What if I do not know the words I should
A3 C4 D4 E4 | G4 F4 E4 -

So I will leave a little room inside the song
C4 E4 F4 G4 | A4 G4 F4 E4
```

## Ending Gesture

The final "Answer when you like" should not resolve too completely. End on C4 in the father part, then let the imagined son answer once on G4.
