# Before Your Name - Song Brief

## Source

- Story source: `docs/story-arc.md`
- Placement: Opening, pre-birth
- Function: Father's pre-birth want song
- Dramatic action: The father tries to prepare for a child he cannot yet know.
- Musical device: Voice/Guitar 1 plays alone, leaving rests where an answer might someday enter.
- Turn: He realizes readiness is partly fantasy.

## Dramatic Need

The father wants to become ready by preparing the room, the house, the guitar, and himself. Underneath the practical action, he is afraid that skill in music and repair will not translate into skill at fatherhood.

## Musical Brief

- Singer: Father
- Absent presence: Son, represented only by rests and imagined response space
- Suggested key: C major with suspended tones
- Suggested tempo: 100 BPM
- Form: Verse 1, Verse 2, Chorus, Bridge, Final Chorus/Tag
- Motif state: Voice/Guitar 1 alone, with deliberate unanswered spaces
