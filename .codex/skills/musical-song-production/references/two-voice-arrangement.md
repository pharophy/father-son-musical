# Two-Voice/Guitar Arrangement

## Voice/Guitar 1: Father

- Early show: mature lead, controlled dynamics, reliable tempo, harmonically stable voicings.
- Middle show: teacher and structure, sometimes too much control.
- Late show: fewer notes, more space, accompaniment as love.

## Voice/Guitar 2: Son

- Early show: absent, imagined, pulse, echo, single note.
- Childhood: simple call and response, rhythmic play, mistakes that become ideas.
- Adolescence: interruption, syncopation, dissonance, volume, new harmonic vocabulary.
- Departure: leads or transforms the theme.

## Track Conventions

- `Voice/Guitar 1 - Father`
- `Voice/Guitar 2 - Son`
- `Guide Melody`
- `Harmony Bed`
- `Bass`
- `Pulse`

Each arrangement should name the current relationship: imagined answer, first echo, imitation, lesson, rupture, restraint, handoff, or coexistence.
