# Lyric Writing Rules

- Start from dramatic need, not theme statement.
- Use singable syntax and repeatable hooks.
- Let verse details become chorus meaning.
- Avoid generic lines like "I will always love you" unless made specific by image or action.
- Keep the rhyme flexible. Forced rhyme is worse than no rhyme.
- Let subtext do work: fathers can talk about fixing, tuning, packing, or timing when they mean fear.
- Mark sections clearly: Verse, Pre-Chorus, Chorus, Bridge, Tag, Reprise, or Instrumental.
- For the father, favor concrete acts of preparation, repair, timing, and restraint.
- For the son, evolve language over the show from fragments and echoes toward independent phrasing.
- Include a short note after the lyric explaining the dramatic turn.
