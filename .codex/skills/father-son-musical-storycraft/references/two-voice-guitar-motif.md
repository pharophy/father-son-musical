# Two-Voice/Guitar Motif Rules

## Voice/Guitar 1: Father

- Starts with mature lead vocabulary, reliable time, richer chords, and controlled bends or vocal phrasing.
- Often carries harmony, structure, and protection.
- Can overplay, dominate, or fill silence when afraid.
- Gradually learns restraint, listening, and accompaniment.

## Voice/Guitar 2: Son

- Enters after birth as a small echo, open-string pulse, hummed interval, or single repeated note.
- Learns by imitation first, then interruption, then counterpoint.
- Gains rhythmic confidence before emotional fluency.
- In adolescence, develops speed, dissonance, volume, or harmonic choices the father does not understand.
- By the departure sequence, can complete, invert, or surpass the father's original theme.

## Relationship Progression

- Before birth: only Voice/Guitar 1, with imagined space for an answer.
- Infancy: Voice/Guitar 2 appears as breath, pulse, or fragile echo.
- Childhood: call and response, simple imitation, playful mistakes.
- Learning years: father demonstrates, son repeats, then changes one note.
- Adolescence: overlapping lines, clashes of tempo, distortion, key changes, or unresolved cadences.
- Departure: Voice/Guitar 2 leads; Voice/Guitar 1 supports or chooses silence.
- Finale: the two lines coexist without hierarchy, or the son's line carries the father forward.
