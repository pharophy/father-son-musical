# Storycraft Workflow

## For New Material

1. Locate the request within the lifecycle arc: pre-birth, birth, early childhood, learning, adolescence, rupture, departure, or independence.
2. Identify the dramatic question of the moment.
3. Define each character's want, fear, tactic, and blind spot.
4. Decide whether the moment is book, song, underscoring, reprise, instrumental exchange, or silence.
5. Define what changes by the end of the moment.
6. Update `docs/story-arc.md` in the relevant sections.
7. Add a dated revision note.

## For Revisions

1. Read `docs/story-arc.md` first.
2. Identify affected continuity: premise, character arc, lifecycle beat, act structure, song map, motif progression, and revision notes.
3. Preserve unaffected decisions.
4. If a request breaks the core premise, state the conflict and propose a compatible option.
5. Apply the smallest coherent update that satisfies the user.
6. Record the change in `Revision Notes`.

## Musical-Theatre Checks

- Character want: What does the character need right now?
- Obstacle: Who or what prevents it?
- Turn: What discovery, choice, or wound changes the scene?
- Song pressure: Why can this not stay spoken?
- Reprise value: What earlier material returns with changed meaning?
- Ensemble value: Who else can embody family, time, memory, school, or community pressure?
- Ending image: What does the audience see or hear that makes the change legible?
